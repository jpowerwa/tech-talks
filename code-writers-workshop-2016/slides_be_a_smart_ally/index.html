Five SMART Goals for Becoming a Better Ally for Gender Diversity in Tech

What is a SMART goal?

  • Specific

  • Measurable

  • Achievable

  • Relevant

  • Timely

What is an ally?

1. A person, group, or nation that is associated with another or others for some common cause or purpose.

ally (n)

2. A person who associates or cooperates with another; supporter.

Five SMART Goals

Goal 1: Ask your friends in tech

At their company, how many women are...

  • Engineers? Out of how many total engineers?

  • Managers?

  • Executives?

  • Board members?

Goal 2: Recruit women

  • Volunteer for the next recruiting event targeted to women.

  • If there are no such upcoming events, find one.

Goal 3: Ask your manager

  • New parent policy?

  • Policies on flex-time, part-time, job sharing, use of sick leave to care for family members?

  • When an employee takes time away from work to care for others, how does that choice impact their chances for career advancement?

Goal 4: Play meeting bingo

Goal 5: Listen to a woman

  • Invite a colleague or friend to coffee and ask about her experiences as a woman in tech.

  • Listen.

  • Don't talk.

  • Listen.

  • Continue to not talk.

  • Listen some more.

Q&A

Is this all on the men?

No, not exclusively, but...

Will working toward these goals really make me a better ally?

Yes, if you open yourself to the resulting experiences.

Is working toward these goals the most efficient way to become a better ally?

Maybe. Maybe not.

If I accomplish these goals, will I be done?

No, but you will have begun.

If I know in my heart that I am an ally, isn't that worth something?

No. It's not what you think, it's what you do.
